Section 01

Introduction

Background and motivation for the project.

Brain tumors are among the most critical health challenges worldwide, with over 300,000 new cases diagnosed annually according to the World Health Organization. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is the most widely used imaging modality for detecting abnormal brain tumors. Traditionally, MRI images are examined manually by trained radiologists — a process that is inherently time-consuming, subjective, and prone to human error.


The emergence of Computer-Aided Diagnosis (CAD) systems using Artificial Intelligence and Deep Learning has opened the door to automated, consistent, and fast analysis of medical imagery. This project, NeuroScan AI, leverages attention mechanisms with convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to classify brain MRI scans and employs image processing techniques using OpenCV to visually detect and highlight tumor regions.


The system is delivered as a modern web application enabling users — clinicians, researchers, or students — to upload MRI scans and receive instant classification results alongside a visual overlay of detected tumor areas and the full step-by-step processing pipeline.

Section 05

Proposed Methodology

🧠
Part 1 — Classification: ACU-Net Channel Attention Modules

Attention mechanisms reuses a model trained on one task as the starting point for another. ACU-Net — pre-trained on ImageNet (14M images, 1000 classes) — is adapted for binary brain tumor classification by replacing its final layer.

StageDescriptionLearning Rate
Stage 1 — FrozenOnly the final classifier layers are trained; the convolutional backbone remains frozen. Leverages pre-learned feature extraction.1e-2
Stage 2 — UnfrozenEntire network is unfrozen and fine-tuned with a very small learning rate to adapt filters to MRI-specific features.1e-5

Implementation Details

🔬
tumor_detector.py — Detection Pipeline
def detect_tumor(image_path, output_dir):
    image = cv2.imread(image_path)
    image = cv2.resize(image, (500, 590))      # Standardise dimensions

    # Step 1 – Grayscale
    gray = cv2.cvtColor(image, cv2.COLOR_BGR2GRAY)

    # Step 2 – Binary Thresholding (value: 155)
    _, thresh = cv2.threshold(gray, 155, 255, cv2.THRESH_BINARY)

    # Step 3 – Morphological Operations
    kernel = cv2.getStructuringElement(cv2.MORPH_RECT, (10, 5))
    closed = cv2.morphologyEx(thresh, cv2.MORPH_CLOSE, kernel)
    closed = cv2.erode(closed, None, iterations=14)
    closed = cv2.dilate(closed, None, iterations=13)

    # Step 4 – Canny Edge Detection (auto thresholds)
    canny = auto_canny(closed)

    # Step 5 – Contour Detection (filter area > 500 px)
    contours, _ = cv2.findContours(canny, cv2.RETR_EXTERNAL,
                                    cv2.CHAIN_APPROX_SIMPLE)
    significant = [c for c in contours if cv2.contourArea(c) > 500]

    # Step 6 – Draw Overlay (red contour + semi-transparent fill)
    cv2.drawContours(image, significant, -1, (0, 0, 255), 2)
    overlay = image.copy()
    cv2.drawContours(overlay, [largest], -1, (0, 0, 255), -1)
    image = cv2.addWeighted(overlay, 0.3, image, 0.7, 0)
